UnInhibited Zombie

Analyzing the evolution of Linkin Park's music over the years

// under python pandas data-analysis

Like a majority of urban teenagers, Linkin Park was one of my favourite rock bands during my high school years. Of course, you might have discovered them later or earlier than that. The moods and emotions that their music captures resonates with different age groups, although it is during our teen years that a lot of us initially experience these emotions.

Linkin Park's music has progressed a lot of over the years, from the radio friendly nu-metal days of Hybrid Theory and Meteora, to alternative rock of Minutes to Midnight, experiments with electronic music on A Thousand Suns, and returning to a heavier rock sound in The Hunting Party. One More Light, their latest album, contains pop elements as well. In this post, we'll try to capture the progression in their style through data analysis on their songs, using Python and Pandas. If you're not a programmer, you can still get a closer understanding of one of your favourite artists by steering clear of the code and focussing on the graphs and insights.

The data I'm using is provided by the very awesome Spotify API. Here is the script I used to get all the data. I'm using a python wrapper for this API, called, Spotipy (I mean, there could have been no other name for it). The API provides meta data for all of their songs - like duration, loudness as well as aggregated content features like Energy, loudness, Instrumentalness (I will be explainging these features as we go on).

This post consists of 4 parts. We'll start by defining our dataset and the features we have available for LP's songs (Henceforth, Linkin Park would be referred in this post as LP). Then we look at some aggregate properties of each album, to understand the albums on a high level. Then, we'll move on to studying some individual albums, and hope to study specific examples of songs. Finally, we'll try to understand how the general "mood" of the albums have changed over the years.

The code for this blog post is available on this github repository

Dataset¶

Let's start by loading our data and seeing what features we have.

We have data for 85 songs from LP's 7 studio albums. That data contains name and album of the song, along with the following features:

  • Energy - a number between 0 and 1 which represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity
  • Tempo - Tempo of the track, in BPM
  • Duration - Duration of the track, in minutes
  • Loudness - Average loudness of the track in decibels
  • Valence - A measure from 0.0 to 1.0 describing the musical positiveness conveyed by a track.

The Spotify API provides a number of additional features for songs - like acousticness, danceability, instrumentalness and time signature. Check out the docs to find out more about this.

Voila! Song lengths didn't vary much in the first two albums, while there was a switch in Minutes to Midnight, where they started varying song length. You must have noticed this, too : the first two albums seemed very formulaic, with similar length songs. Minutes to Midnight had some songs which broke the 3 minute barrier which LP had till then - Shadow of the Day was at around 5 minutes, and the album closer, "The Little Things Give You Away", at 6.4 minutes. A Thousand Suns was their most "varied" album (makes sense, because as far as I know, its their only concept album. In fact, Christopher Weingarten of The Village Voice compared it to Radiohead's OK Computer), with short songs (interludes?) like "The Radiance" and "Empty Spaces" and longer ones like "The Catalyst". They lowered down the variance in Living Things, and then bumped it up again in Hunting Party (which, although returned a bit to the heavier rock style of the first two albums, did not return to their original style of less variant songs). One more light, was, I guess a big stylistic switch in terms of being more "poppy" in nature, and it also returned to the variation of the first two albums

Energy and Loudness¶

One of the major switch in stype of LP's albums over the years have been with respect to the raw energy and intensity. The earlier albums were clearly more raw and intense, while after that, there was more variation, and mixture of intensity versus "softness" in their songs. We try to capture this phenomen through the "energy" and "loudness" parameters of each song.

Lets group the albums to make it easier to analyze them. Hybrid Theory and Meteroa into one group, the next two albums, Mniutes to Midnight and A Thousand Suns into another, Living Things and Hunting Party into another, and finally, One More Light

Valence¶

LP has always been a "dark" band, dealing with negative issues. But how does this vary across their albums? Are some less negative than others? We can get more insights on this by looking at the "valence" feature in our dataset.

According to the spotify API docs, valence is:

A measure from 0.0 to 1.0 describing the musical positiveness conveyed by a track. Tracks with high valence sound more positive (e.g. happy, cheerful, euphoric), while tracks with low valence sound more negative (e.g. sad, depressed, angry).
